Day 1 · Lab · Part 1 · ~45 min

Train Your First AI Model 🚦

No code needed — you will collect data, train an image classifier, and evaluate it. The full lifecycle, compressed into one exercise.

The three ingredients of AI

  • Data — the foundation; models learn from large amounts of examples
  • Algorithms (models) — mathematical models that find patterns and make decisions
  • Computing power — GPUs that crunch the data and run the algorithms

Today you provide the data, and Google's Teachable Machine provides the model and the compute — through a friendly no-code interface.

Guided demo (with the instructor)

Together we train a traffic-sign classifier with 3 classes, 100 images each:

  • 🚫 "Stop" — 100 images of stop signs
  • ⬆️ "Go Straight or Turn Right" — 100 images
  • 🚗 "80 km/h" — 100 images of the speed-limit sign

Open teachablemachine.withgoogle.com → Image Project → follow along.

📦 Get the data firstDownload the lab datasets (zip, 1.6 MB) and unzip. Inside: Traffic Sign Datasets (the 3 demo classes, training + testing) and Practice Opportunity Data (adds Turn Left and Wildlife Crossing for your exercise).

⚠️ Access note (China)Teachable Machine is a Google service and may need the classroom VPN. If it is unreachable, follow the instructor's projector demo and do the exercise with Baidu EasyDL — the workflow (upload classes → train → test) is the same.

🏆 Exercise — two new traffic signs

Now on your own (or in pairs): train and evaluate a classifier that recognises two additional traffic signs — (1) Turn Left and (2) Wildlife Crossing.

  1. Data collection: in the lab datasets zip, open Practice Opportunity Data → Training Data: it has the images for both new classes (plus the 3 demo classes).
  2. Model development: create an image project, upload both classes, and train with the built-in tools.
  3. Model evaluation: test with the images in Testing Data (3 held-out images per class). Record every prediction and its confidence.

Analyze: Which sign is recognised more reliably? When the model fails, what do the failing images have in common (angle, lighting, background)?

💡 Experiment like an engineerRetrain with fewer epochs (e.g. 10 instead of 50) or a much higher learning rate. What happens to accuracy? This is the Modeling ↔ Evaluation loop of the lifecycle — you just lived it.

✅ What you just practised

Data quality beats model cleverness: the same tool gives very different results depending on what you feed it. Remember this on Day 3, when we design data strategy for real systems.
