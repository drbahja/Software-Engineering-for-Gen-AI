The Price of Intelligence 💰

What does it really cost to train a model like GPT-4 or DeepSeek? Research it — and learn why almost nobody trains from scratch.

The scenario

You are consultants advising a mid-size tech company that wants to build a generative AI system. The client asks: "What would it take to train a model like GPT-4 from scratch — and is that financially realistic for us?"

Your job: research the real numbers and deliver an evidence-based answer.

How to research like a consultant (10 min)

  1. Pick ONE frontier model to investigate: GPT-3, GPT-4, Gemini 1.5, Claude 3 — or, very relevant here, DeepSeek-V3/R1 (famous for training at a fraction of US costs!).
  2. Search for evidence, not opinions. Good queries: "GPT-4 training cost estimate", "DeepSeek V3 training cost 5.5 million", "[model] GPUs used training". Good sources: company technical reports, Epoch AI, Stanford AI Index, reputable tech press.
  3. Record the source next to every number. A number without a source is a rumour. If sources disagree, write the range.
  4. Distinguish one-time vs ongoing costs: training happens once; inference (serving users) costs money every single day.

💡 Worked example rowModel: DeepSeek-V3 · Estimated training compute cost: ~$5.6M (official technical report, widely debated) · Chips: ~2,048 × NVIDIA H800 · Note: excludes staff, research, prior experiments — the "true" total is higher. See how each claim carries its source and a caveat? That's the standard.

The consultant's answer: training from scratch is for a handful of companies on Earth. Everyone else fine-tunes existing models or builds on top of APIs (prompting, RAG, agents) — exactly the skills of this course. Even DeepSeek's famous "$5.6M" run sits on top of hundreds of millions in accumulated hardware and research.

💡 Discussion to close the dayDeepSeek claims ~50× cheaper training than GPT-4 estimates. What does that mean for who can afford to compete in AI — and for what YOU can build with the API access you already have?

✅ What you just practised

Evidence-based cost research and the single most important strategic fact in Gen AI: build on top of models, don't train them. That's exactly what we do for the rest of the course.
