Spot the Challenges 🕵️

Real AI projects fail in predictable ways. Learn to see the traps before you fall into them.

Scenario: real-time fraud detection

You are a data scientist at a fintech company. Your team must build an AI model that detects fraudulent credit-card transactions in real time, so suspicious charges can be blocked before they cause damage.

Sounds straightforward? Every single lifecycle step hides a trap. Your job is to find them.

🏆 Exercise — one trap per step

In pairs, for each of the 7 lifecycle steps, write down:

  1. a key challenge you would face at that step,
  2. its impact on fraud detection,
  3. a practical solution.

Think about: What makes fraud data special? How often do fraudsters change tactics? What happens if the model is slow? Is "accuracy" a good metric when 99.9% of transactions are legitimate?

🔓 Reveal the model answer (only after you finish!)
StepChallengeImpactSolution
1. Problem ScopingFraud team and data team disagree on what counts as "fraud"Wasted labeling effort, inconsistent outcomesDefine fraud types with domain experts; align on KPIs (% fraud blocked)
2. Data AcquisitionPrivacy laws (GDPR) limit access to real fraud examplesIncomplete training data → unreliable modelAnonymised data; synthetic data for rare fraud types
3. Data ExplorationExtreme imbalance: 99.9% legit vs 0.1% fraudModel classifies everything as "normal"Resampling, anomaly detection, cost-sensitive learning
4. ModelingFraud patterns change quicklyStatic models become obsolete fastOnline learning or frequent retraining
5. EvaluationUsing accuracy instead of precision/recallToo many false alarms, or missed fraudRecall to catch fraud + precision to avoid false alarms
6. DeploymentComplex models are slowDelayed alerts, unhappy customersLow-latency, lightweight models for real-time use
7. MonitoringFraud tactics keep evolvingModel loses effectiveness over timeMonitor, retrain weekly, human-in-the-loop review

💡 Connect it backLook at your diabetes plan from Part 1: which of these traps apply there too? (Hint: imbalance, privacy, and drift almost always do.)
